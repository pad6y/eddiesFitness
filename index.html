Physiological training plan

This is essential for the person centred way in which I work. Everyone’s fitness abilities are different so it’s about tuning into the individual, assessing their capabilities and designing a plan that maximises their potential and helps achieve fitness goals in a structured, disciplined and progressive way, usually though a mixture of cardiovascular and weight training exercises.

If fitness is not followed in a structured and proper way then the whole purpose will be defeated. It’s important individuals have the correct lifting weights, are working on a specific area of their body each training day, are completing the right amount of sets/reps, are having days off at the right time (when they should complete appropriate stretching exercises), and that the cardiovascular exercise is at the right intensity (often run/walk intervals are a good starting point for this).

My training plan typically covers an initial 15 week period split into three 5 week sections which gradually become increasingly more challenging to improve the individual and always look to push them out of their comfort zone.

starting at £30

Nutrition plan

You can’t outrun a bad diet! There is no point having an effective physiological training plan without an effective nutrition plan to compliment it.

My nutrition plans cover 4 weeks and includes the 4 meals of the day, snack ideas and recommendations for portion sizes. They take account of individual preferences, I always ask the customer to let me know foods they DON’T like so that I leave these out of the 4 week plan.

The nutrition plans include the perfect mix of carbohydrates, protein and healthy fats. Some of the meal combinations may look a little strange/complicated but I always advise that customers give them the benefit of the doubt, most are pleasantly surprised! And all the recipes are on Google and NOT as complicated as they seem!

As with all aspects of sport science, I closely monitor the very latest in healthy nutrition to compliment a structured training plan, trying to ensure that nutrition is fun, varied and above all else tasty!

starting at £30

Sport psychology support

This is my favourite sport science principle and an area I studied in great depth at university and I continue to keep abreast of developments in this field.

My sport psychology support consists of a meeting with each individual customer to get an idea of how I can help them with this aspect. Following on from this is 3-4 weeks of sport psychology support techniques/mindset changes done over text/email followed by another meeting with the customer to assess how they felt the support had been for them and if any aspect of their sport psychology support requires further explanation.

Training and nutrition is nothing if the mind isn’t in the right place for exercise so this is another essential part of the fitness jigsaw.

1:1 training sessions

This is where all of the above support is actually put into practice! All of my sessions are done outside and on a 1:1 basis.

I seek to encourage customers through a supportive approach during sessions. Sport psychology techniques may be used but I am not an authoritarian trainer, I look to positively encourage and develop customers through positive reinforcement and constructive criticism.

Sessions will be analysed and assessed in order to ascertain how a customer is developing. Questions are welcome at any time, no such thing as a silly question!
